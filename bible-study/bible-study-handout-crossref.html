📚 How the Themes Connect Across Both Passages

👁️ Jesus Sees
Before We Ask
John 6:5–6 — Feeding the 5,000

Jesus sees the crowd and their need. He tests Philip: "Where shall we buy bread?" — but He already knows what He will do. He sees ahead of time.

Matthew 14:13, 22 — Sending and Staying

Jesus "saw the crowds" and had compassion. Then He sent the disciples ahead while He dismissed the crowds. He sees both the crowd's need and the disciples' storm.

Psalm 139:1–4

"You have searched me and known me. You know my sitting down and my rising up..." God sees before we call.

🍞 God's Abundance
vs. Human Scarcity
John 6:7–13 — Five Loaves, Twelve Baskets

200 denarii ($30K) wasn't enough — but a boy's lunch was. Scarcity thinking (Andrew) vs. Jesus who multiplies what seems impossible.

2 Kings 4:1–7 — Elisha and the Widow's Oil

The widow's oil was "not enough to pay her debts" — but God multiplied it until the jars ran out. Same pattern: scarcity to abundance.

Ephesians 3:20

"Now to Him who is able to do immeasurably more than all we ask or imagine, according to His power that is at work within us."

🌊 He Walks
in the Storm
John 6:19–21 — Walking on the Sea

The disciples had rowed 3–3.5 miles against a harsh wind. Then they saw Jesus walking on the sea — and immediately, they reached their destination.

Matthew 14:24–27 — "Be of Good Cheer"

Jesus walks toward them in the storm — not after it passes, but during it. "Be of good cheer; it is I; be not afraid."

Isaiah 43:2

"When you pass through the waters, I will be with you; and through the rivers, they shall not overwhelm you."

🦶 Stepping Out
in Faith
Matthew 14:28–31 — Peter Walks

Peter asked to be called out. Jesus said "Come." Peter got out — and walked. He sank because he took his eyes off Jesus and looked at the waves.

Joshua 1:9

"Have I not commanded you to be strong and courageous? Do not be frightened, and do not be dismayed, for the LORD your God is with you."

James 1:6–8

"He who doubts is like a wave of the sea, blown and tossed by the wind. That person should not suppose they will receive anything from the Lord."

🙏 Worship
After Revelation
Matthew 14:32–33 — "Truly, Son of God"

After the storm, after walking on water, after witnessing the miracle — the disciples fell down and worshipped: "Truly, You are the Son of God."

John 6:14–15 — Wrong Worship

The crowds wanted to make Jesus king by force. Worship motivated by what He could do, not who He is. Jesus withdrew.

Romans 1:20

"Since the creation of the world, His invisible attributes have been clearly seen..." Creation reveals — we respond in worship.

📖 Side-by-Side: What Each Gospel Emphasizes

Event / Theme 📘 John's Emphasis (Ch. 6) 📗 Matthew's Emphasis (Ch. 14)
Jesus' compassion He tests Philip — He already knows what He'll do. His provision is deliberate. He "saw the crowds and had compassion on them" — then healed their sick.
The miracle Detailed: 5 loaves, 2 fish, 12 baskets. Everyone ate and was full. More than enough. The miracle is assumed — emphasis is on who Jesus is, not the mechanics.
Jesus' divinity "I am the Bread of Life" — later in the chapter (v. 35, 48). Connected to feeding. "Truly, You are the Son of God" — disciples' confession after witnessing power.
Jesus' withdrawal He "withdrew again to the mountain by Himself" — after avoiding being made king. He "went up on the mountain by Himself to pray" — sent disciples ahead into the storm.
Peter Not mentioned walking on water in John's account. Called out of the boat, walks on water, sinks, is saved — full story.

🔗 More Cross-References to Explore

Mark 6:45–52

Mark's account of walking on water — includes detail that Jesus "meant to pass by them."

Luke 9:10–17

Luke's feeding account — includes Jesus taking, blessing, breaking, and giving. Same Greek word used of the Last Supper.

John 6:35 — "I Am the Bread of Life"

Later in John 6, Jesus unpacks what the miracle meant: He is the bread that came down from heaven. The crowd ate bread but still didn't understand.

Exodus 16 — Manna in the Wilderness

God provided bread from heaven (manna) in the desert. Jesus reclaims this title — He is the true bread. The feeding miracle echoes and fulfills the exodus pattern.

Matthew 6:25–34 — Do Not Be Anxious

Jesus' teaching on anxiety directly connects: "Your Father in heaven knows that you need them. Seek first the kingdom..." The feeding miracle is a living sermon on this.

Habakkuk 3:19

"God, the Lord, is my strength; He makes my feet like the deer's; He makes me walk on my high places." — walking on water is a picture of walking in trials victoriously.
