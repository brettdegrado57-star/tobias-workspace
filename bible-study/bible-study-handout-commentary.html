📖 Commentary & Reflection Guide

John 6:1–15 & Matthew 14:22–33 | Feeding the 5,000 and Walking on Water

Part 1: The Feeding of the 5,000 — John 6:1–15

📋 Key Observations

  • v. 2 — Large crowds were following Jesus because they saw the miraculous signs He was doing on the sick.
  • v. 5 — Jesus looks up and sees the crowd. He sees their need before they ask. He tests Philip: "Where shall we buy bread?"
  • v. 6 — Jesus already knows what He will do. The test is for them, not for Him.
  • v. 7 — 200 denarii ≈ $30,000 today. Andrew finds a boy with five loaves and two fish but says "what is that among so many?"
  • v. 10 — The crowd sits down. 15,000–20,000 people. Order before miracle.
  • v. 11 — Jesus takes, gives thanks, and distributes. The manner of the miracle is not described — He simply gets the job done.
  • v. 12 — "Gather the pieces that are left over" — nothing is wasted. Twelve baskets collected. More than enough.
  • v. 14–15 — The people want to make Jesus king by force. He withdraws alone to the mountain.

🔑 Key Phrases

"Jesus took the loaves, gave thanks, and distributed to those who were seated." — John 6:11
"Gather the leftover pieces so that nothing will be lost." — John 6:12
"So then, do not be anxious about your life, what you will eat... your Father in heaven knows that you need them." — Matthew 6:25, 32 (cf. Jesus' teaching)

👥 Crowd's Response

  • Followed Jesus for physical healing (v. 2)
  • Satisfied by the miracle but missed the meaning (v. 26)
  • Wanted to make Him king by force — human plan, not divine timing (v. 15)

Part 2: Walking on Water — John 6:16–22 & Matthew 14:22–33

📋 Key Observations

  • v. 17 — disciples get into a boat and head across the lake. It grows dark. Jesus remains behind.
  • v. 18 — A strong wind kicks up. The sea becomes rough.
  • v. 19 — They rowed 3–3.5 miles against the wind. Exhausted. Struggling. Everything crashing down.
  • v. 20 — "It is I; do not be afraid." — Jesus appears on the water in the storm.
  • v. 21 — They willingly take Him into the boat. Immediately, they reach the land.
  • Matt 14:28–29 — Peter asks Jesus to call him out onto the water. Jesus says "Come." Peter walks — until he looks at the waves.
  • Matt 14:30–31 — Peter sinks. "Lord, save me!" Jesus immediately reaches out and catches him.
  • Matt 14:32–33 — They worship: "Truly, You are the Son of God."

🔑 Key Phrases

"Be of good cheer; it is I; be not afraid." — Matthew 14:27 / John 6:20
"Immediately Jesus made His disciples get into the boat... and He went up on the mountain by Himself to pray." — Matthew 14:22–23
"O you of little faith, why did you doubt?" — Matthew 14:31

👥 Disciples' Journey

  • Struggled alone for hours (3–3.5 miles, strong wind)
  • Jesus sees them — just as He saw the crowd's need earlier
  • Worship comes after revelation, not before

🤔 Reflection & Application Questions

On John 6:1–15

  1. What is the "need" in your life right now that feels as impossible as feeding 20,000 people?
  2. How does knowing God already knows your need before you ask change how you approach Him in prayer?
  3. Andrew brought what little he had and Jesus multiplied it. What is the "five loaves and two fish" in your hand right now that feels too small?
  4. The crowd wanted Jesus for what He could do for them. What motivates your pursuit of Jesus — His benefits, or Himself?
  5. Jesus withdrew from the crowds who wanted to make Him king. What does this teach us about avoiding the approval of people?

On Matthew 14:22–33

  1. Have you ever felt like you were "rowing hard" but getting nowhere? Describe that season.
  2. Jesus says "Be of good cheer." In the middle of your current storm, what would it look like to actually have good cheer? Is that even possible?
  3. Peter asked Jesus to call him out — he didn't wait to be asked. What is God calling you to step out into right now?
  4. Peter sank when he took his eyes off Jesus and looked at the waves. What are your "waves" that pull your focus away from Christ?
  5. Jesus didn't rebuke Peter for sinking — He caught him immediately. What does this reveal about how Jesus responds to your weakness and doubt?
  6. Both the crowd and the disciples ended in worship — but different kinds. What's the difference between being amazed by Jesus and actually worshipping Him?
