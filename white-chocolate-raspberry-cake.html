🍫 White Chocolate & Raspberry
Cookie Layer Cake

Assembled like tiramisu — no bake, chilled, layered

🍪 COMPONENT 1 — White Chocolate Cookie Layers
White Chocolate Cookie Layers

Buttery, crumbly, vanilla-forward with subtle white chocolate flavor — 3 layers

All-purpose flour2 cups (280g)
Baking soda½ tsp
Fine salt½ tsp
Unsalted butter, room temp1 cup (226g)
Brown sugar, packed¾ cup (150g)
Granulated sugar½ cup (100g)
Large egg, room temp1
Pure vanilla extract2 tsp
White chocolate, melted and cooled8 oz (226g)
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line sheet pans with parchment.
  2. Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt together. Set aside.
  3. Cream butter and both sugars on medium-high for 4–5 minutes until fluffy and pale.
  4. Beat in egg and vanilla until combined.
  5. Beat in melted white chocolate until smooth.
  6. Gradually add flour mixture on low until just combined. Do not overmix.
  7. Divide dough into thirds. Wrap each in plastic. Refrigerate 45 minutes.
  8. Roll each portion between two sheets of parchment to ¼-inch thick.
  9. Bake 10–12 minutes until edges are set but centers look slightly underdone. They firm up as they cool.
  10. Cool completely on pan before assembling.
Pro tip: For clean 8-inch rounds, roll and bake on a pizza stone. Cut circles with the base of an 8-inch springform pan while cookies are still warm.
🧈 COMPONENT 2 — White Chocolate Mascarpone Filling
White chocolate, finely chopped12 oz (340g)
Heavy cream¾ cup (180ml)
Mascarpone cheese, room temp16 oz (454g)
Powdered sugar, sifted½ cup (65g)
Pure vanilla extract2 tsp
Fine salt¼ tsp
Raspberry coulis (optional — for color)2 tbsp
  1. Place white chocolate in a heatproof bowl.
  2. Heat cream until just simmering. Pour over chocolate. Let sit 2 minutes.
  3. Stir from center outward until completely smooth and glossy. Cool to room temperature (about 30 minutes).
  4. Beat mascarpone, powdered sugar, vanilla, and salt on medium speed until smooth and fluffy.
  5. Fold in the cooled white chocolate ganache in two additions. Fold in coulis for color if using.
  6. Refrigerate until firm enough to spread (about 30 minutes).
🍓 COMPONENT 3 — Raspberry Coulis
Fresh or frozen raspberries12 oz (340g)
Granulated sugar¼ cup (50g)
Fresh lemon juice2 tbsp
Vanilla paste½ tsp
Fine saltPinch
  1. Combine raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir and cook until raspberries break down and mixture slightly thickens, about 8–10 minutes.
  3. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve, pressing to extract all liquid. Discard seeds.
  4. Stir in vanilla paste and salt. Cool completely.
  5. Should coat a spoon but still pour. If too thick, add 1 tbsp warm water. If too thin, cook 2 more minutes.
🍓 COMPONENT 4 — Fresh Raspberry Filling
Fresh raspberries, picked over3 cups (340g)
Raspberry coulis2 tbsp
Fine saltPinch
  1. Toss gently with coulis and salt just before assembly. Do not prepare ahead.
🍫 COMPONENT 5 — White Chocolate Ganache Glaze
White chocolate, finely chopped10 oz (283g)
Heavy cream¾ cup (180ml)
Unsalted butter, room temp1½ tbsp
Vanilla extract½ tsp
Fine salt1 pinch
  1. Heat cream until just simmering. Pour over white chocolate.
  2. Let sit 2 minutes, then stir until perfectly smooth.
  3. Beat in butter and vanilla until glossy. Cool until pourable but not runny (about 15–20 minutes at room temperature).
🍽️ Assembly
Tools needed: 8-inch springform pan · Acetate sheets or parchment strips · Offset spatula
🍪 Step 1 — Cookie Layer 1

Place one white chocolate cookie round in the bottom of the springform pan. If it cracks, press pieces together — it will hold.

🧈 Step 2 — Filling Layer 1

Spread half of the white chocolate mascarpone filling evenly over the cookie.

🍓 Step 3 — Coulis Drizzle 1

Spoon half of the raspberry coulis in a loose spiral over the filling. Don't spread it — let it sit.

🍓 Step 4 — Fresh Raspberries 1

Scatter half of the fresh raspberries over the coulis.

🍪 Step 5 — Cookie Layer 2

Add the second cookie round. Press gently.

🧈 Step 6 — Filling Layer 2

Spread the remaining filling evenly.

🍓 Step 7 — Coulis Drizzle 2

Spoon the remaining coulis in a spiral.

🍓 Step 8 — Fresh Raspberries 2

Scatter the remaining fresh raspberries.

🍪 Step 9 — Cookie Layer 3 (Top)

Add the third cookie round. Press very gently.

🍫 Step 10 — Ganache Glaze

Pour the white chocolate ganache over the top. Use an offset spatula to spread to the edges and let it drip down the sides naturally.

🍓 Finishing Touches

Top with fresh raspberries, white chocolate curls, and a light dusting of freeze-dried raspberry powder (optional).

🧊 Setting & Serving
⏰ Chill

Cover with plastic wrap pressed directly onto the surface. Refrigerate minimum 6 hours, preferably overnight. This is what sets everything — the cookies soften slightly, the filling firms, and the layers fuse.

🔪 Serve

Run a thin knife around the edge of the springform. Release. Wipe knife between each cut for clean slices. Bring to room temperature 15 minutes before serving.

⭐ Pro Tips
Overnight is non-negotiable. This is a chilled-set cake. Slicing before it's set will make a mess. Plan ahead.
Room temperature mascarpone. Cold mascarpone will not fold smooth — it'll be lumpy. Take it out of the fridge 30 minutes before starting.
White chocolate quality matters. Use Valrhona Ivoire, Callebaut, or Guittard. Do not use white candy coating — it won't melt the same way.
The coulis must be completely cool before using, or it'll melt the filling.
If ganache sets too firm, warm the bottom of the bowl in warm water for 10 seconds and stir.
📦 Storage

Refrigerate up to 4 days. Do not freeze — the cookie layers will lose their texture.
