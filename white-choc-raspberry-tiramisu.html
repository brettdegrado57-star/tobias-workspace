White Chocolate & Raspberry Tiramisu

Assembled like tiramisu — no bake, chilled, layered

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour — 2 cups (280g)
  • Baking soda — ½ tsp
  • Fine salt — ½ tsp
  • Unsalted butter — 1 cup (226g), room temperature
  • Brown sugar — ¾ cup (150g), packed
  • Granulated sugar — ½ cup (100g)
  • Large egg — 1, room temperature
  • Pure vanilla extract — 2 tsp
  • White chocolate — 8 oz (226g), melted and cooled (for cookies)
  •  
  • White chocolate — 6 oz (170g), finely chopped (for filling)
  • Heavy cream — ¾ cup (180ml)
  • Mascarpone cheese — 16 oz (454g), room temperature
  • Powdered sugar — ½ cup (65g), sifted
  • Pure vanilla extract — 2 tsp
  • Fine salt — ¼ tsp
  •  
  • Fresh or frozen raspberries — 8 oz (227g) (for coulis)
  • Granulated sugar — ¼ cup (50g)
  • Fresh lemon juice — 2 tbsp
  • Vanilla paste — ½ tsp
  • Fine salt — pinch
  •  
  • Fresh raspberries — 2 cups (170g) (for filling)
  • Raspberry coulis — 2 tbsp
  •  
  • White chocolate — 4 oz (113g), finely chopped (for ganache glaze)
  • Heavy cream — ¼ cup (60ml)
  • Unsalted butter — 1½ tbsp, room temperature
  • Vanilla extract — ½ tsp

White Chocolate Cookies

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line sheet pans with parchment.
  2. Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt together. Set aside.
  3. Cream butter and both sugars on medium-high for 4–5 minutes until fluffy.
  4. Beat in egg and vanilla until combined. Beat in melted white chocolate until smooth.
  5. Gradually add flour mixture on low until just combined. Do not overmix.
  6. Divide dough into thirds. Wrap each in plastic. Refrigerate 45 minutes.
  7. Roll each portion between two sheets of parchment to ¼-inch thick.
  8. Bake 10–12 minutes until edges are set. Cool completely on pan.

White Chocolate Mascarpone Filling

  1. Place 6 oz white chocolate in a heatproof bowl.
  2. Heat ¾ cup cream until just simmering. Pour over chocolate. Let sit 2 minutes.
  3. Stir from center outward until smooth and glossy. Cool to room temperature (about 30 minutes).
  4. Beat mascarpone, powdered sugar, vanilla, and salt on medium speed until smooth.
  5. Fold in the cooled white chocolate ganache in two additions.
  6. Refrigerate until firm enough to spread (about 30 minutes).

Raspberry Coulis

  1. Combine raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Cook, stirring, until raspberries break down and mixture slightly thickens, about 8–10 minutes.
  3. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve, pressing to extract all liquid. Discard seeds.
  4. Stir in vanilla paste and salt. Cool completely.
  5. Should coat a spoon but still pour. Add 1 tbsp warm water if too thick.

Fresh Raspberry Filling

  1. Toss 2 cups fresh raspberries gently with 2 tbsp coulis and a pinch of salt.
  2. Prepare just before assembly — do not do ahead.

White Chocolate Ganache Glaze

  1. Heat ¼ cup cream until just simmering. Pour over 4 oz white chocolate.
  2. Let sit 2 minutes, then stir until perfectly smooth.
  3. Beat in butter and vanilla until glossy. Cool until pourable but not runny.

Assembly

Tools: 8-inch springform pan · Parchment strips

Cookie 1 — Place one cookie round in the bottom of springform. Press pieces together to cover.

Filling 1 — Spread half the mascarpone filling evenly.

Coulis 1 — Spoon half the raspberry coulis in a spiral. Do not spread.

Raspberries 1 — Scatter half the fresh raspberries.

Cookie 2 — Add second cookie round. Press gently.

Filling 2 — Spread remaining filling.

Coulis 2 — Spoon remaining coulis in a spiral.

Raspberries 2 — Scatter remaining fresh raspberries.

Cookie 3 (Top) — Add third cookie round. Press very gently.

Glaze — Pour white chocolate ganache over top. Spread to edges and let it drip down the sides.

Topping — Fresh raspberries, white chocolate curls.

Setting & Serving

Chill — Cover with plastic wrap pressed onto surface. Refrigerate minimum 6 hours, preferably overnight. This firms everything up — do not skip.

Serve — Run a thin knife around edge of springform. Release. Slice with a hot, dry knife. Bring to room temperature 15 minutes before serving.

Note: Overnight rest is non-negotiable. The cookies absorb moisture from the filling and the layers fuse. Slicing too early = mess.
White chocolate quality matters. Use Valrhona Ivoire, Callebaut, or Guittard — not white candy coating.
Mascarpone must be room temperature before mixing, or it will be lumpy. Take it out 30 minutes before starting.
