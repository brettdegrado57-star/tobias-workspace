Priority Use Cases

Primary

1. Dynamic Risk & Policy Alignment Agent

The problem: Every year, banks undergo regulatory risk assessments. Each department then writes policies against that assessment — but nobody has visibility across all departments to ensure alignment. Policies get siloed. Risk assessments and actual policy language drift apart without anyone catching it.

What the agent does: Reads and monitors all policies, procedures, and standards across every department. Continuously compares them against the current risk assessment and flags where alignment breaks down — before regulators do.

Why only an agent can do this: No single human reviews all policies across all departments. The volume and cross-departmental nature of this task makes it impossible to do manually and error-prone if done sporadically.

Primary

2. Autonomous Internal Controls Tester

The problem: Controls are tested on a predictable schedule. Teams know when testing is coming — they prepare, tighten things up, and get a false picture of compliance. The current approach tests compliance, not actual control effectiveness.

What the agent does: Runs concealed, randomized tests throughout the year (e.g., three times, unannounced). Tests blindly — no prep, no warning. Evaluates whether controls actually work, then delivers a detailed report: what failed, why, and how to improve it.

Why this is better than the current process: A third-party, unbiased, always-on tester that produces genuinely corrective insight — not just a compliance checkbox.

What a Pilot Would Look Like

A pilot keeps scope tight and proves the concept before firm-wide commitment:

  • Scope: One client scenario or one internal workflow — specifically either the Alert Triage use case (below) or a single policy review cycle using the Risk & Policy Alignment agent
  • How it runs: Agent works in parallel with current process. Compare outputs side-by-side. Measure accuracy, time savings, and analyst experience.
  • What you need: Access to one live or historical data set, and a security review of the tool before it touches client data
  • What you get: A proof-of-concept — does it work, does it save time, is the output usable? — that you can present back to leadership with real numbers
  • Timeline: Depends on KPMG's internal security review process — typically 2–8 weeks for approval, 4–6 weeks to run the pilot itself

Additional Use Cases (Proof of Breadth)

  • Alert Triage & Prioritization: Reviews the full case queue and ranks cases by risk level — analysts start with the highest-risk cases first, rather than working FIFO
  • SAR Narrative Generation: Pulls structured data and generates a first-draft narrative for SAR filings — analyst reviews and refines, doesn't start from scratch
  • Adverse Media Screening: Monitors and synthesizes news and public records across clients on a continuous basis, flagging material changes
  • Digital Investigator / Level 1 Reviewer: Automates initial transaction review and alert investigation for low-risk cases, escalating only what requires human judgment
  • Perpetual KYC Agent: Continuously monitors client profiles and refreshes KYC data outside of periodic review cycles
  • UBO Unraveling Agent: Traces complex ownership structures to identify ultimate beneficial owners — especially valuable for shell company detection
  • Sanctions Alert Resolution: Provides contextual analysis of sanctions hits to accelerate and improve analyst decision-making
  • Internal Fraud Simulation: Models potential fraud scenarios against internal controls to identify vulnerabilities proactively
